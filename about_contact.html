I'm Washim Shaikh, a Computer Science Engineering student with a habit of going further than the assignment requires. My work sits at the intersection of software engineering and applied AI — I'm less interested in theory for its own sake and more focused on building systems that hold up when they hit real data, real users, and real constraints.

Over the past couple of years I've built across a range of domains — an e-auction platform for farmers that cuts out the middleman entirely, a fraud detection pipeline that flags suspicious transactions in real time, a Vision Transformer trained from scratch on CIFAR-10, and healthcare-grade models for brain tumor classification and driver drowsiness detection. The problems are different, but the approach is the same: understand the domain first, then engineer a solution that's clean and maintainable, not just functional.

I've completed internships in AI & Python at Coincent, Machine Learning at Yhills, and Full Stack Development at 1Stop. I'm currently gaining cloud expertise through an ongoing AWS internship at iStudio. My goal isn't to build impressive demos — it's to build things that are genuinely useful.
